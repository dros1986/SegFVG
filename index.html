SegFVG dataset

Claudio Rota, Flavio Piccoli, Rajesh Kumar, Gianluigi Ciocca
University of Milano-Bicocca
Nature Scientific Data 2025
A high-resolution dataset for semantic segmentation of building footprints from aerial orthophotos with 0.1 m/pixel ground sampling distance.
Paper Download dataset

Abstract

Accurate building extraction from high-resolution aerial imagery is critical for applications in remote sensing, urban planning, and disaster management. AI-based methods enable fast, scalable, and cost-effective segmentation of building footprints, but their development is often limited by the lack of geographically diverse, large-scale datasets with reliable pixel-level annotations. In this work, we present SegFVG, a large-scale dataset for building segmentation focused on the Friuli Venezia Giulia, a region in northeastern Italy. The dataset comprises over 15,000 true orthophoto aerial image tiles of size 2000x2000 pixels, with a ground sampling distance of 0.1 meters. Each tile is paired with pixel-level building segmentation masks. SegFVG covers an extended area of about 616 km2 and captures diverse urban, suburban, and rural environments across complex geographic landscapes, spanning from mountainous to coastal areas. In addition to the dataset, we provide benchmark results using different deep learning models, which serve as a baseline for future research.
